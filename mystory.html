Lycée Mixte - My High School

Mathematical Foundation

The Beginning of Resilience

When I was just seven years old, a political coup erupted in my country, turning life upside down overnight. My family and I had to flee our city without my father and without any means of transportation—only our legs and the belongings we could carry on our backs. My father had to hide because rebels were forcing able-bodied men to join their army, and those who refused faced death.

With courage and determination, my mother led us as we walked miles until we found someone who could take us to Yamoussoukro, the nearest city. That city became my home throughout most of my school years. Every morning, I walked over two hours to attend classes, but I never allowed hardship to take away my joy. I laughed, played with friends, and dedicated myself to learning. Math and physical sciences quickly became my favorite subjects.

Discovering Excellence

During my middle school years, something happened that completely shaped my confidence and pursuit of excellence. In a "classe d'excellence"—a special group for top students—we faced a challenging practice exam. The questions were so difficult that even our teacher struggled to find the answers. Hours passed, and no one could solve the hardest problem. With patience and determination, I worked through the challenge, and the solution finally came to me. I volunteered to present it on the board. As I wrote out my answer, my classmates applauded, and my teacher nodded in approval. That day, I discovered the power of persistence, courage, and believing in myself.

Academic Excellence

That spirit carried me forward. I passed my national middle school exam (BEPC) with outstanding scores—17/20 in both math and physics—during a year when more than 82% of students failed nationwide. In high school, I continued striving for excellence, ranking 1st in my class for the last two years of high school, with an average of 18/20 in math and physics.

Higher Education and Mission

After high school, I pursued a degree in Mathematics and Physics at university. In my sophomore year, I made the difficult decision to pause my studies and serve a full-time mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. As the first in my family to attend college, it was a sacrifice for both me and my parents, but it deepened my resilience and purpose.

Pursuing Data Science

When I returned in 2019, I resumed my studies and earned my Bachelor's degree, ranking among the top 20 out of more than 500 candidates. I then began a Master's program in Statistics, but during that time I felt a strong call to pivot toward a career in Data Science. I made the bold decision to leave my Master's program and move to the United States to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Data Science—a step that required my family and me to invest everything we had.

My Philosophy

This journey taught me that excellence is not a destination but a quest—if we truly seek it, we will find it.

✨ Takeaways

This story isn't just mine—it's a reminder that resilience, adaptability, and the courage to take risks are the same qualities that help us grow in our careers and in life. Whether in a workplace project or a personal challenge, perseverance and a commitment to excellence can turn obstacles into opportunities.

Personal Life

My family background and the values that shape my professional approach

I come from a family of seven, and being the second-to-last taught me early on the importance of respecting and collaborating with others. Growing up with four older sisters and two brothers, I learned how to listen, compromise, and work as part of a team—skills that naturally carried into my academic and professional life.

Although my family had meager means, my parents instilled in us the values of love, care, and mutual respect. Complaining was never part of our mindset. Instead, we focused on finding solutions and believing that challenges were opportunities in disguise. I often say that difficult moments are "worth gold" because they teach us lessons that shape who we are. This resilience and problem-solving mindset are qualities I bring into every workplace challenge.

In September 2025, I married my wonderful wife, and today my family is my number one priority. This commitment grounds me and fuels my drive to succeed. It reminds me daily of the importance of responsibility, integrity, and long-term thinking—values that I apply to my work as well.
